About Me

Who am I?

I'm Jai Prakash, a medical student with a passion for technology and web development.

I'm deeply passionate about building impactful digital solutions. What began as a curiosity—designing simple static pages—has transformed into a full-stack development journey focused on solving real-world problems.

I specialize in crafting dynamic, responsive websites and configuring robust server infrastructures. Over time, my skills have evolved to architect seamless user experiences and scalable backend systems.

Outside of medicine, I find joy in coding, experimenting with emerging technologies, and mentoring aspiring developers. My mission is to bridge creativity and code, turning ideas into meaningful web experiences.

I'm currently pursuing my MD (MBBS) at Georgian National University (SEU), where I'm deepening my understanding of medical science, clinical reasoning, and patient care.

My journey in medicine complements my technical background, enabling me to tackle healthcare challenges through an integrated lens of science and technology.

I'm passionate about leveraging digital solutions to improve medical education, healthcare delivery, and patient outcomes. With this dual foundation, I aim to bridge the gap between medicine and innovation.

Outside of lectures and clinical practice, I actively build web-based tools that intersect with healthcare, from patient management systems to educational platforms—exploring how technology can transform lives both inside and outside the hospital.
